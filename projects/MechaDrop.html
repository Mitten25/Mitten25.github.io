MechaDrop

Space Based King of the Hill Survival

Primary Roles: Game Design, Backend Engineer, UI/Front End Engineer, and Sound


Year: Fall 2016


Platforms: MS Windows, MAC OS


Tools and Technologies: Unity, Adobe Illustrator, Adobe Photoshop, Maya


Description: Mechadrop is meant to be a frustrating couch competative shooter where players work together to keep each other from winning. To begin, the players are dropped into a free for all. The winner of this fight starts in the Mech! This free for all causes the ship holding the players to explode, causing them to suddenly start falling towards a planet's surface. Players must fight for the Mech as only the player inside will survive impact. When in the Mech players have new abilities that essentially make them the boss. The other players must work together to kill this boss, with one catch. Only the player who dealt the most damage will trade places for the Mech. Mechadrop finds a great blend between competition and teamwork, with most games coming down to the very end.

Mechadrop was my first introduction to the Unity engine and C#. The majority of my gameplay programming centered around player movement and abilities. Player's have many tools at their disposal. These include, their primary rifles, a reflective shield, a dash, and a melee attack. Mech abilities include two attacks, a charging plasma ball, and a high fire rate machine gun. Game design was a challenge steming from decisions on balancing and winning strategies. My first experience with UI also came in Mechadrop, where I designed in-depth menus and a character select. Mechadrop was a great first team project where I learned many of the basic game creation skills I have today.
